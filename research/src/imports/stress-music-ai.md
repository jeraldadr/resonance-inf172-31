Background and Significance
Teenagers are experiencing increasingly high levels of stress and anxiety. National surveys show that teens report an average stress level of 5.8 out of 10, far above what they consider healthy (3.9) and even higher than adults (5.1) (American Psychological Association, 2014). Recent studies also indicate that adolescent anxiety and related mental-health disorders have been rising significantly in prevalence (Anderson et al., 2024). This mental-health burden emphasizes the need for accessible tools that help young people understand and manage their stress. Since music plays a major role in teens’ daily routines and has documented effects on mood regulation, it represents a promising intervention pathway.
Our project addresses this need by developing an AI-powered mood classifier and personalized music recommendation system that uses self-reported stress levels, emotional context, and daily tasks to suggest existing music that supports well-being. This approach integrates mental-health research, youth behavior, and AI-supported decision tools to create a simple, scalable resource for stress management.
Building on this foundation, focusing on specifically teenagers and college students is important because this age group is navigating academics, stress, and developmental stressors at the same time. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, rates of persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, and stress-related symptoms among adolescents and young adults have increased steadily over the past decade, with especially sharp increases observed after 2015. These trends suggest that younger populations are not only experiencing higher stress, but may also lack effective coping strategies during critical periods of emotional and cognitive development (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2021).
This problem highlights the challenge of translating subjective emotional states, such as stress, mood, and motivation, into structured data that can be meaningfully interpreted by AI systems. Understanding how self-reported emotional inputs and contextual factors (such as time of day or task type) can inform adaptive recommendations raises important questions at the intersection of human-centered design and mental-health research. Practically, this work is significant because existing mental-health resources are often inaccessible, stigmatized, or reactive rather than preventative, especially for younger users.
By leveraging music, which is an already embedded part of teens’ and college students’ daily lives, this project aims to provide a low-barrier, non-clinical tool for stress awareness and emotional regulation. Rather than replacing professional mental-health care, the proposed system serves as an early, supportive intervention that encourages intentional coping behaviors. 
2. Existing Solutions and Gap Analysis

There are existing solutions at the intersection of music and AI that aim to regulate mood. These AI-powered applications are capable of translating natural language into emotional data, recommend music or create playlists based on user data, and even generate its own music. These solutions come from both academia and industry, with each one having its own strengths and weaknesses when it comes to the goal of mood regulation, stress reduction, and personalized listening experiences. 

One example is PersonalAIs, a conversational AI that is capable of recommending music based on mood and natural language prompts. This system specifically excels at interfacing with a large language model, equipping it with tools in order to interact with Spotify and the user at the same time. 

Other commercial applications include Endel, BetterSleep, and Noisli, which all focus on supporting relaxation, focus, and sleep through music. These applications feature a list of customizable sounds and noises that can be mixed together to create the user’s perfect soundscape. Endel leverages AI in order to generate these soundscapes to suit the user’s mood. Physiological data such as heart rate is also taken into account when generating soundscapes.

In addition to commercial applications, academic research involving the use of AI-generated music has been explored as well. Context-AI Tune, or CAT for short, is an AI-powered system that is able to generate personalized music with the goal of reducing stress (Smith et al., 2025). The system then is able to take user input and produce music. A study was conducted with this system to investigate the effectiveness of AI generated music intervention. The results of the study indicated that users who listened to music generated by CAT experienced a greater reduction in stress than users who listened to manually chosen music. Academic research has also been done on reducing stress through music interventions in general. There is substantial evidence showing that music interventions significantly reduce both physiological and psychological stress markers (de Witte et al., 2020). This research suggests that these solutions that utilize music interventions are effective at reducing stress and regulating mood, which is what they aim to do.

While these projects have effective approaches towards the solution, there are still gaps that remain to be filled. In terms of our project, our goal is to combine these solutions into one cohesive project that utilizes music intervention to reduce stress. We plan to include unique features such as a way to visualize stress patterns using user data, allowing users to see how their stress levels change over time in response to the music being recommended. We also plan to add a community feature, allowing users to share music and connect with others on the platform through discussion forums. By fostering this sense of community, we aim to provide a platform that allows users to promote their well-being together and regulate emotional health as a whole. 

3. Proposed Solution 

To address the rising stress levels among teenagers and college students, we propose a web-based, AI-powered stress awareness and music recommendation platform designed specifically around daily routines, emotional patterns, and social contexts. Creating a web-based platform will allow users to access the system across different devices without requiring any downloads, ensuring accessibility for our target audience in their daily routines. This system focuses on preventative and supportive stress management. 
 
The proposed application will collect data from users through self-reported stress inputs that include perceived stress level (low, medium, high), emotional state/mood, task type (studying, exercising, etc.), and date/time. Using these inputs, this data will function as a mood classifier that analyzes patterns over time. Specifically, the AI component will interpret self-reported mood and stress inputs to estimate emotional context. It will then match emotional states and task types with music features associated with either relaxation, focus, or emotional regulation. It can provide playlist suggestions that align with either short term needs (e.g. a cool down during exam stress) or long-term habits. The input-to-output flow of this recommendation system creates a low-friction user experience that follows a familiar or predictable pattern, allowing for an experience that can be integrated seamlessly into users’ everyday activities.

Users will also be able to identify and visualize their stress patterns with graphs and self-reported data reports. The purpose of these surveys are to give users a low-pressure check-in system that makes stress tracking feel natural and accessible rather than intrusive. The platform will also provide users with visual dashboards showing stress levels over time, correlations between stress and day-to-day activities, and trends during high-pressure periods. These visualizations will translate raw data into tangible patterns, helping users build self-awareness around their stress patterns and encourage proactive coping behaviors. Rather than creating a tool for the purpose of crisis-response, this platform is designed to support long-term growth and help users identify warning signs early on and adjust their habits accordingly.

To future distinguish our solution, we will include a community tab within the application where users can share playlists they enjoy and are associated with a specific mood or tasks, or discover playlists created by users with similar music or stress patterns. Beyond individual recommendations, the AI system will foster a socially supportive environment by identifying shared patterns and music preferences to help similar users connect with each other. By connecting users through music, this platform supports social belonging and emotional validation, which are important for teenagers and college students navigating academic and development stressors.
